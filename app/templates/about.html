The Origins of Terrible Captionists

Aliza and Carl. You can devise a better caption.

In January 2015, Aliza's friend Carl set out to win the New Yorker Cartoon Caption Contest by entering every week for a year. To keep motivated—and amused—he corralled all his funniest friends to enter each week with him. Carl, Aliza and the rest of the self-named "Terrible Captionists" shared their captions each week in a group email, and together, grew funnier. (Carl ended up a finalist in the real contest.)

In January 2015, Aliza decided she was going to finally learn how to program. She decided a good project would be an app to support the contest, and get it out of the email inbox. (In January 2016, Aliza finally built it.)
